Dupuytren's disease involving Guyon's canal.

Juvenspan M(1), Schlur C(2), Thomsen L(2), Colom A(2), Audren JL(2).

Author information: 
(1)Clinique La Montagne, 10, rue de la Montagne, 92400 Courbevoie, France;
Institut français de chirurgie de la main, 5, rue du Dôme, 75116 Paris, France.
Electronic address: m.juvenspan@free.fr. (2)Clinique La Montagne, 10, rue de la
Montagne, 92400 Courbevoie, France.

Ectopic localization of Dupuytren's contracture is extremely rare. To the best of
our knowledge, there have been only six cases reported in the literature. The
authors present a case with Dupuytren's contracture involving Guyon's canal where
the patient had limited wrist range of motion and ulnar neuropathy.
